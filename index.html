Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, and a blend of aromatic spices. This flavor-packed recipe gives you that smoky, party-style taste everyone loves.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 40 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 5 medium tomatoes (blended)
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 red bell peppers (blended)
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and seasoning cubes to taste

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly and set aside.
  2. Blend tomatoes, red peppers, and part of the onion until smooth.
  3. Heat oil in a pot, sauté onions, and stir in the tomato paste.
  4. Add the blended mixture, seasonings, curry, and thyme. Cook until the sauce thickens.
  5. Add the chicken stock and rice, stir well, then cover and cook on low heat.
  6. Allow the rice to absorb all the sauce, stirring occasionally to avoid burning.
  7. Once soft and smoky, turn off the heat and fluff the rice with a fork.

Tips

For the perfect smoky flavor, let the rice slightly burn at the bottom for a few minutes — that’s the famous “party Jollof” aroma! Serve with fried plantain or grilled chicken.
